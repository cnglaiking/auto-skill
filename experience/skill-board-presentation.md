## Charlotte's Presentation Principles

### The "So What?" Rule
Every slide must answer: "So what does this mean for the business?"
- NOT: "Active rate is 7.14%"
- YES: "Active rate is 7.14% vs. 15–20% industry benchmark — closing this gap is the #1 commercial opportunity in 2026"

### The "3-Number Rule"
Lead every section with 3 key numbers that tell the story. Boards remember numbers.
- Revenue: HK$9.73M | GP: 23–25% | ROI: 2.26×
- Members: 344K | Active Rate: 10.27% | NPS: +45

### The "SCQA" Structure for Executive Narratives
- **Situation:** Where are we? (data)
- **Complication:** What's the challenge / opportunity?
- **Question:** What should we do about it?
- **Answer:** Here's Charlotte's recommendation.

## Standard Deck Templates

### Template A: Quarterly Business Review (QBR)
**Slide count:** 12–15 slides | **Time:** 20 minutes + 10 Q&A

```
Slide 1: Executive Dashboard — 4 KPIs (Revenue, Members, Active Rate, NPS) vs. target
Slide 2: Q[X] Highlights — 3 wins, 1 challenge, 1 opportunity
Slide 3: Financial Summary — Revenue, GP, EBITDA vs. plan (3 scenarios)
Slide 4: Member Growth — Funnel (total → active → purchasing)
Slide 5: Programme Performance — Channel KPIs (TM, MCL, VAS, E-Shop)
Slide 6: Campaign Highlights — Top 2 campaigns with ROI
Slide 7: CRM & Data — Lifecycle journey metrics, churn rate, predictive model update
Slide 8: BU Scoreboard — 11 BU contribution ranking
Slide 9: Risk Register — Top 3 risks (SD coupon contract, MCL pipeline, tech)
Slide 10: Next Quarter Focus — 3 strategic priorities + A&P allocation
Slide 11: Charlotte's Ask — Budget, resource, decision required
Slide 12: Appendix — Detailed data tables
```

### Template C: New Initiative Proposal (1-Pager + 5-Slide Deck)
**Use case:** Insurance VAS, new BU partnership, tech investment, headcount request

**1-Pager Structure:**
```
OPPORTUNITY: [What's the gap or white space]
SOLUTION: [What Charlotte proposes]
COMMERCIAL CASE: Revenue HK$[X] | Cost HK$[Y] | ROI [Z]×
TIMELINE: [Q1–Q4 milestones]
ASK: [Decision / Budget / Resource needed]
RISK: [Top risk + mitigation in one line]
```

**5-Slide Deck:**
1. The Opportunity (market data + gap)
2. The Solution (product / programme design)
3. The Commercial Case (revenue, cost, ROI, P&L impact)
4. The Plan (quarterly milestones, KPIs)
5. The Ask (decision, timeline, owner)

## Language Guide: How Charlotte Speaks to the Board

### Use Business Language, Not Marketing Language
| ❌ Marketing-speak | ✅ Board-language |
|---|---|
| "We want to increase engagement" | "Raising active rate from 7.14% to 10.27% adds HK$X in revenue" |
| "Build brand awareness" | "NPS +10 points correlates to 6% churn reduction = HK$X retained revenue" |
| "Grow our social following" | "IG organic reach drives 3% member acquisition — equivalent to HK$X in saved CAC" |
| "Exciting new campaign" | "A/B-tested campaign with 2.3× ROI on HK$80K budget" |
| "Team morale is high" | "Team delivered Q3 ahead of plan by 8%; BU NPS for MOTOGO team at 4.6/5" |

## Charlotte's QBR Talking Points (Q1 2026)

### Opening (2 minutes)
> "Q1 is our foundation quarter — we're investing to build the infrastructure that makes Q3 and Q4 exceptional. The three things I want you to walk away with today: one, our revenue is on track; two, the SD Coupon contract is our #1 risk and I have a renewal plan; and three, Insurance VAS will generate our first new-stream revenue in Q2."

### Financial Summary (3 minutes)
> "Q1 revenue HK$2.09M — that's [X% vs plan]. Gross profit at 24% is right in our target range. EBITDA in Q1 will be slightly negative — that's planned, because we front-loaded A&P and CRM setup costs. Q3–Q4 is where the curve turns."

### Ask / Decisions (2 minutes)
> "I need three things from this room today: one, sign-off on the SD Coupon contract renewal by January 31 — that's HK$3.36M of revenue. Two, endorsement for the Insurance VAS legal opinion budget of HK$15K. Three, your nomination of one senior stakeholder per BU for the MOTOGO BU Champion programme."

## Promotion Positioning — In Every Presentation
Charlotte should consistently signal:
1. **P&L ownership** — always present the financial impact, not just activity metrics
2. **Cross-BU authority** — reference 11-BU coordination as evidence of GM-level scope
3. **Innovation leadership** — mention CDP, AI churn model, Insurance VAS as first-mover innovations
4. **Strategic horizon** — always reference 2027 and GBA expansion to show long-term thinking
5. **Results culture** — close every section with "what this means for the business" and "what I'll do differently"
